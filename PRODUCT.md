# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Primary users are individual consumers buying from marketplace sellers in a familiar consumer-commerce context. They want to save time, avoid awkward back-and-forth negotiation, avoid being lowballed or overcharged, and feel safe because the system follows clear rules and human approval is required before commitment.

Secondary users are marketplace sellers who want to respond faster, protect margins, and avoid wasting time with unserious buyers. The product should support their participation without turning the core experience into a seller admin tool.

The core user context is practical, repeatable, everyday commerce: browse a listing, initiate negotiation, review one active negotiation at a time, inspect the reasoning and audit trail, then approve or reject the final deal.

## Product Purpose
DealRoom is a premium marketplace product where AI agents negotiate with sellers on behalf of buyers while remaining transparent, governed, and reviewable. The product is not an AI chat destination; it is a marketplace workflow that uses AI quietly in the background to help buyers reach better deals faster and with more confidence.

Success means users immediately understand that they are browsing real listings, launching a negotiation from a listing, reviewing a structured negotiation timeline, and approving a final deal only after the terms, checks, and tradeoffs are clear. The product should make negotiation feel safer, faster, and more professional than typical marketplace haggling.

The first core workflows are:
- Marketplace discovery
- Agent configuration for buyer and seller rules in a demo context
- Single negotiation review, one negotiation at a time
- Final deal review with price, terms, seller details, and Workato verification
- Transparent audit trail with chronological events, market checks, validation, and approval history

## Brand Personality
Trustworthy, professional, transparent, efficient, and premium.

The interface should inspire confidence and feel realistic enough for real purchases. It should feel like a modern consumer marketplace with strong product polish, where AI operates quietly in the background rather than acting as the main character of the interface.

## Anti-references
- ChatGPT-style conversation interfaces
- Claude-style chat layouts
- Generic AI SaaS dashboards
- Vercel AI demo aesthetics
- Glassmorphism
- Purple or pink gradient-heavy design systems
- Neon or cyberpunk visual styling
- Floating glowing cards
- Overly animated interfaces
- Anything that immediately signals "AI startup"

Do not copy Carousell directly, but do preserve the strengths of familiar marketplace products: dense but readable listings, fast browsing, and obvious commerce affordances. Aim closer to the polish and restraint of Airbnb, Linear, Stripe Dashboard, Apple, and Arc, while still feeling like a consumer marketplace first.

## Design Principles
1. Marketplace first, AI second. The product must read as a buying and negotiation platform before it reads as an AI product.
2. Trust through transparency. Every important outcome should be inspectable through clear terms, structured reasoning, verification signals, and a visible audit trail.
3. Professional calm over AI spectacle. Use restraint, consistency, and precise hierarchy so confidence comes from clarity rather than novelty.
4. One negotiation at a time. The main product experience should focus the user on a single live negotiation thread with structured state, not a dashboard of simultaneous chats.
5. Human approval before commitment. The interface should repeatedly reinforce that agents assist and negotiate, but users remain in control of final decisions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG 2.2 AA is the baseline.

The product must include strong text contrast, visible focus states, full keyboard navigation, reduced motion support, consistent interaction patterns, and readable typography and spacing. Accessibility should improve trust and usability without making the interface feel sterile, enterprise-heavy, or overly utilitarian.
